The Problem

Why static defenses fail

Detection engineering is a reactive discipline. Rules are written after incidents, tested once, and forgotten. Attackers study those rules and stay outside their patterns.

Coverage Gap

You can't detect what you can't see

The average enterprise SIEM detects 21 of every 100 MITRE ATT&CK techniques. That means for 79 techniques an adversary can use right now, your SIEM has no rule to catch it — and most teams don't know which 79.

Rule Decay

Rules go stale in weeks

Volt Typhoon lived in US critical infrastructure for years — not through zero-days, but because they knew what detection rules look for and stayed outside those patterns. Static rules are a published roadmap for evasion.

Episodic Testing

Annual red teams don't close gaps

A $150K red team report lands on a desk. 90% of the findings are never implemented because detection engineers are busy. Between engagements, new gaps open. The cycle continues.

The Solution

A continuous arms race inside your SIEM

Two AI agents fight 24/7. One attacks. One defends. Every miss generates a new rule. Every catch forces a mutation. Coverage improves every round.

R

Red Agent injects synthetic ATT&CK-mapped attacks

Realistic log events — identical to real attacker telemetry — injected into index=arena_attacks via Splunk HEC with realistic timing spread.

↓
B

Blue Agent executes Sigma detection rules

Every rule runs in parallel against the injected data. Each technique is scored hit/miss with a kill-chain dwell timer — did detection happen before the attacker achieved their objective?

↓ (on miss)
B

Blue generates a new Sigma rule via LLM

The missed attack events are passed to an LLM with the existing rules as context. A new Sigma YAML rule is generated, compiled to SPL, and queued for human review before promotion to production.

↓ (on catch)
R

Red mutates to evade — and remembers

Red's Mutation Inferencer reads Blue's active ruleset and learns what patterns Blue is watching. Future attack templates route around those patterns. Red gets harder as Blue gets better.

↓
✓

Coverage improves — measurably, exportably

After each run, an ATT&CK Navigator JSON layer shows exactly which techniques are now detected, which rules cover them, and the before/after delta. Ready to present to a CISO or auditor.
